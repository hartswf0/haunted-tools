Mission & Purpose

Mission: Equip communities to resist hegemonic / NGO extraction.

Purpose: Build critical literacy and counter‑design capacity.

Design stance: power‑aware, community‑led, refusal‑compatible. Use the components below as prompts to co‑produce strategy, not as off‑the‑shelf solutions.

Components (Collapsible)

01 Cultural Probes ↕
  • Photo/Audio diaries that map extraction touchpoints (forms, checkpoints, metrics).
  • “Power postcards”: participants mail a card to future selves naming red lines & non‑negotiables.
  • Shadow the metric: log every time a KPI distorts care; collect counter‑metrics.
  • Refusal kit: script phrases for declining harmful participation (say no without losing voice).
02 Activities ↕
  • Map the money: diagram where funds flow, who chooses, who benefits; publish a one‑page zine.
  • Contra‑brief: rewrite the client brief from the community’s standpoint; add harms & safeguards.
  • Borrowed agency audit: list all actors claiming to speak for the community; validate consent.
  • Design embargo: define conditions under which work pauses until accountability is met.
03 Postcolonial Readings ↕
  • Spivak — Can the Subaltern Speak? (representation vs. re‑presentation).
  • Escobar — Encountering Development (development as discourse & apparatus).
  • Tuck & Yang — Decolonization Is Not a Metaphor (against settler moves to innocence).
  • hooks — Talking Back (pedagogies of voice & liberation).
04 Movement References ↕
  • Participatory action research (PAR) & community tech zines.
  • Mutual aid networks: governance templates, care agreements, forkable bylaws.
  • Design justice principles (DJN) adapted as local charters; track accountability.
  • Union toolkits: grievance flows, shop‑floor surveys, strike school basics.
